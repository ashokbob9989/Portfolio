About Me

Roots and Early Life

I was born and brought up in Jangaon, Telangana (506302), in a farming family where discipline, resilience, and perseverance were part of daily life. Growing up in an agrarian household shaped my outlook early, teaching me the value of consistent effort and grounded thinking.

Schooling and Foundation

I completed my education up to Class XII through government welfare institutions, including Tribal Welfare and Social Welfare schools. These institutions provided not only academic support but also a structured environment that emphasized responsibility and self-discipline.

The Turning Point

During my higher secondary education, one of my physics lecturers played a pivotal role in my academic journey. His mentorship helped me understand the rigor and mindset required to prepare for competitive examinations such as IIT JEE Main and Advanced, and it was here that my approach to learning became more focused and goal-driven.

Self-Study and Determination

With limited resources, I relied heavily on self-study, borrowing books from friends and building my preparation independently. This phase strengthened my ability to learn autonomously, manage time effectively, and remain consistent under constraints.

Choosing the Right Path

Driven by a strong interest in Computer Science and Engineering, I made a conscious decision to prioritize my domain of interest over institutional branding. I chose NIT Durgapur for CSE, even when IIT options were available in non-CSE disciplines, believing that depth in the right field mattered most.

Competitive Programming Journey

I regularly competed on platforms such as CodeChef, Codeforces, AtCoder, HackerRank, LeetCode, and Google Kick Start. These experiences sharpened my algorithmic thinking and helped me build a strong problem-solving mindset.

Leadership and Community

One of my proudest achievements in college was being selected as a community contributor for RECursion, the official programming community of NIT Durgapur. With only 10-15 contributors selected, this role allowed me to mentor peers, contribute to community initiatives, and strengthen the coding culture on campus.

Industry Transition

These cumulative experiences prepared me for real-world software development, leading to internships and eventually my current role as a full-time Software Engineer at The Bank of New York Mellon (BNY). Today, I work on building reliable and scalable software systems while continuously refining my technical and professional skills.
